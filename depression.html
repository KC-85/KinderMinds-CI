Understanding Depression

Understanding Depression

Depression can be experienced in various forms, affecting everyday life, relationships, and overall well-being.

Common Types of Depression

Common Types of Depression

Clinical Depression

Clinical depression is diagnosed by a doctor and may require professional treatment.

Recurrent Depressive Disorder

This disorder involves repeated depressive episodes and often needs long-term treatment.

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

Those with SAD experience depressive episodes that align with particular seasons or weather conditions.

Depression Support and Treatments

Support and Treatments for Depression

Available Medications

  • SSRIs: fluoxetine, sertraline, citalopram
  • SNRIs: duloxetine, venlafaxine
  • MAOIs: phenelzine, isocarboxazid
  • Tricyclics: amitriptyline, clomipramine

These medications may help relieve symptoms but can have side effects.

Therapies

Options include Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), counseling, and mindfulness practices.
