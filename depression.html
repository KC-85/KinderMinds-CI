Depression (Kinder Minds)

Depression can be experienced in lots of different ways...

Some commonly diagnosed types of Depression are:

Clinical Depression

Clinical depression means that a doctor has given you a diagnosis of depression.

Recurrent Depressive Disorder

If you've had at least 2 depressive episodes, your doctor might say that you have a recurrent depressive disorder...

Seasonal Affective Disorder

If you have SAD, you'll experience depression during particular seasons, or because of certain types of weather...

Depression Support

Available Treatments for Depression

There are various evidence-based treatments or therapies that have been found to help with Depression & Depressive disorders...

Medication

SSRI

  • fluoxetine (Prozac or Oxactin)
  • sertraline (Lustral)
  • dapoxetine (Priligy)
  • vortioxetine (Brintellix)
  • escitalopram (Cipralex)
  • citalopram (Cipramil)
  • paroxetine (Seroxat)
  • fluvoxamine (Faverin)

SNRI

  • duloxetine (Cimbala)
  • venlafaxine (Efexor)

MAOI

  • phenelzine
  • isocarboxazid
  • tranylcypromine

These medications cause potentially serious side effects. Should only be prescribed by specialist doctors.

TCAs

  • amitryptiline
  • lofepramine
  • clomipramine
  • dosulepin
  • nortryptiline

These are just some of the available Medications for Depression (Same medications also work for Anxiety).

Other Available Treatments

Talking Therapies

  • Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT)
  • Applied relaxation
  • Exposure and response prevention (ERP)
  • Hypnotherapy
  • Meditation
