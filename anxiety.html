Anxiety (Kinder Minds)

Anxiety can be experienced in lots of different ways. If your experiences meet certain criteria your doctor might diagnose you with a specific anxiety disorder.

Some commonly diagnosed anxiety disorders are:-

Social Anxiety Disorder

It's a common problem that usually starts during teenage years. It is a long-term and overwhelming fear of social situations which can be distressing and can have have a big impact on your life. Itâ€™s normal to worry about social or performance situations. social anxiety disorder, also known as social phobia, is when you will have an intense fear or dread of social situations. This can happen before, during or after an event.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder

You may be given this diagnosis if your anxiety problems involve having repetitive thoughts, behaviours or urges which have an impact in your daily life.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

PTSD & C-PTSD can cause a wide variety of physical, mental and emotional symptoms such as hypervigilance, irrational anger & fear, panic attacks, flashbacks, digestive issues, feeling numb, nightmares and exhaustion.

Available Treatments for Anxiety

There are various evidence-based treatments or therapies that have been found to help with anxiety and panic disorders. This page will cover the many resources available to us now.

Medication.

SSRI

  • fluoxetine (Prozac or Oxactin)
  • sertraline (Lustral)
  • dapoxetine (Priligy)
  • vortioxetine (Brintellix)
  • escitalopram (Cipralex)
  • citalopram (Cipramil)
  • paroxetine (Seroxat)
  • fluvoxamine (Faverin)

SNRI

  • duloxetine (Cimbala)
  • venlafaxine (Efexor)

MAOI

  • phenelzine
  • isocarboxazid
  • tranylcypromine

These medications cause potentially serious side effects
Should only be prescribed by specialist doctors.

TCAs

  • amitryptiline
  • lofepramine
  • clomipramine
  • dosulepin
  • nortryptiline

These are just some of the available Medications for Anxiety.
