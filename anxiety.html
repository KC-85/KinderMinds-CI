Anxiety

Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety can be experienced in many different ways. If your experiences meet certain criteria, your doctor might diagnose you with a specific anxiety disorder.

Commonly Diagnosed Anxiety Disorders

Social Anxiety Disorder

Social anxiety disorder is a long-term and overwhelming fear of social situations, which can be distressing and have a significant impact on life.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD involves repetitive thoughts, behaviors, or urges that cause distress and disrupt daily life.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can cause various symptoms, such as hypervigilance, irrational fear, panic attacks, flashbacks, and more.

Anxiety Support and Treatments

Medication

Several medications can help with anxiety and panic disorders:

  • SSRI: fluoxetine, sertraline, escitalopram
  • SNRI: duloxetine, venlafaxine
  • MAOI: phenelzine, isocarboxazid
  • TCA: amitryptiline, clomipramine

Note: Some medications, such as MAOIs, may have serious side effects and should only be prescribed by specialists.

Other Available Treatments

There are various talking therapies, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), hypnotherapy, and meditation, that can help manage anxiety.
