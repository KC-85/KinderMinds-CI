Domestic Violence & Mental Health

Domestic violence (DV) can have lasting effects on mental health, including PTSD, anxiety, and depression. Seeking help is the first step toward healing.

Effects of Domestic Violence on Mental Health

  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD): Survivors may experience flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.
  • Depression: Feelings of helplessness, guilt, and worthlessness.
  • Anxiety Disorders: Many survivors develop chronic anxiety, hypervigilance, or panic attacks.

Support & Treatment for DV Survivors

There are multiple support systems available for those affected by domestic violence, including:

  • Therapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and trauma-informed counseling can help survivors regain control over their emotions.
  • Support Groups: Connecting with others who share similar experiences can be a powerful step toward healing.
  • Emergency Assistance: Helplines, legal aid, and shelters are available for immediate help.
  • Self-Care & Mindfulness: Practicing relaxation techniques, exercise, and creative expression can support mental well-being.
