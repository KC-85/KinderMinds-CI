Domestic Violence & Mental Health

Understanding the impact and finding safe, confidential support.

You’re Not Alone

Domestic violence (DV) can affect anyone, regardless of age, background or gender. It can have a profound impact on mental health, including anxiety, depression, trauma responses and low self-esteem. Confidential help is available whenever you’re ready.

Possible Signs

  • Fear of a partner’s reactions or controlling behaviours
  • Isolation from friends, family or finances
  • Monitoring of phone, messages or movements
  • Low mood, anxiety, sleep difficulties
  • Feeling responsible or “walking on eggshells”
  • Changes to work/study/attendance

Safety & Self-Care Ideas

Plan Ahead

Identify safe people and places. Consider a small essentials bag and important numbers saved under neutral names.

Keep Notes Safely

Record incidents or feelings in a safe place (only if it doesn’t increase risk). Clear browser history if needed.

Reach Out

Talk to someone you trust. If you feel in immediate danger, call emergency services.

Get Support (UK)

If you need confidential advice or to discuss options, consider contacting:

  • National Domestic Abuse Helpline – 0808 2000 247 (24/7)
  • Men’s Advice Line – 0808 801 0327
  • Galop (LGBTQ+) – 0800 999 5428
  • Samaritans – 116 123 (24/7)

If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
