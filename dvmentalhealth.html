With Domestic Violence & the associated Mental Health issues which may come with this, can be very traumatic for both men & women alike.
Please remember that help is available & that no matter how isolated you feel, YOU ARE NOT ALONE.

Mental Health issues associated with DV include:-

PTSD

It can be a common problem in some cases that starts during an abusive relationship. It may be a long-term issue that requires either short-term or long-term therapy to help overcome these issues.
It's normal to feel irrational anger, fear, depression & even suicidal in some cases.
Hypervigilance, flashbacks, nightmares may also be issues for you. In which case, please call our number which is on the home page.

Anxiety (OCD)

You may be given this diagnosis if your anxiety problems involve having repetitive thoughts, behaviours or urges which have an impact in your daily life.

Substance Abuse

Sometimes, the trauma brought on by an abusive relationship can cause some people to have an unhealthy relationship with substances such as Alcohol or Drugs.
Help is available for you.

Mental Health Support (DV)

Available Support

There are various evidence-based treatments or therapies that have been found to help people with Mental Health issues relating to Domestic Violence. We will cover some of the resources available to us now.

Therapy.
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy
  • Counselling

    • Counsellors can work with you to help you recognise signs of abuse and help you understand your own behaviours/way of thinking.
    There are many different techniques and approaches that may be able to support you, from assertiveness and grounding techniques to CBT to improve self-esteem.

Any medication that you may be prescribed may be similar or the same as for anxiety/depression.

Rebuilding life after abuse

Once you have left your relationship, it's understandable for there to be mixed emotions.
You may feel a sense of loss and sadness, guilt, overwhelm and/or anger.
You may blame yourself and think you could have 'worked harder' to make the relationship succeed or feel as if you are ‘weak’.
Of course, the truth is, you have been exceptionally courageous and strong, please do not forget this part especially.
